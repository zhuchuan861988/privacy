How to Make A Career Out Of Daily Vlogging

by Saif Abbas | Apr 29, 2020 | Creativity, how to series | 0 comments

A vlog is a video blog. Videos uploaded to the internet on platforms such as YouTube or a blog are referred to as vlogs. Vlogs have become a popular way of disseminating information, and the popularity of this activity has evolved into a massive industry. Vlogs offer a unique way of releasing information to a large number of people in a short time, thanks to the power of the internet.

The rise of web syndicates has also driven the success of vlogs by offering the opportunity of mass distribution. The web syndicates aggregate the content and feed it to personal computers and mobile phones through the internet.

Why Should You Vlog?

For some, vlogging is a hobby, but to others, it is a serious business that has spawned millionaires across the world. When your hobby earns good money, it is time for you to turn it into a big business!

Daily vlogging is not for the fainthearted. It takes considerable work and dedication to produce videos every day, no matter how short they are. The sheer volume of work you need to put into this activity puts off many people or people who start, drop off somewhere along the way. However, it’s not all doom and gloom as anyone on a low budget can start a vlogging career.

Is vlogging daily worthwhile after all this effort?

Well, here are a few observations that will help you make up your mind.

  1. With quality and regular content, platform algorithms pick it up and push it to the top of the pile. The resulting exposure ensures your subscriber base proliferates.
  2. The chance of some of your videos going viral is very high.
  3. The number of views and subscribers translates into higher ad revenues and a greater chance of sponsorship, bringing in sacks of crisp, clean banknotes.
  4. Practice makes perfect. Daily volume improves your filming and video editing skills considerably. You may eventually find yourself directing big-budget movies!
  5. Vlogging is great fun, and making some cash out of it is a bonus!

For ultimate success as a vlogger, here are a few tips you may want to consider:

Make a Plan

Before you embark on your blogging adventure, make a plan. Define what you want to do and what you want to vlog about. Can you be able to do it every day? Can you vlog comfortably for a month? Three Months? One Year? With a well laid out plan, you will notice you will be able to proceed smoothly.

Why are you embarking on this? What’s the structure of your daily vlogging—every day for one month, two months, or one year? You have to define these things so you can know how to make plans and follow them through with ease.

Start Slow

If you’re a newbie, the prospect of vlogging every day may seem daunting, so you may want to start at a slow but steady pace, making regular posts every week for a while. When you settle and get into the rhythm, you may want to pick up the pace and start vlogging daily.

Don’t jump in at the deep end too early, or you may end up producing poor quality stuff that will ruin your career even before you’ve started it. Start slow and build up.

Learn From Others

Don’t be afraid or wary of seeking counsel or looking at what other established vloggers are doing. You will glean valuable insights into the business that may help you on your way. The work of others also offers motivation to put out quality information and enables you to understand that you can do it.

Check out Vlogmas, Veda, Vlog every day of your life, Vlogust or others you may be aware of, and learn from them.

Follow a Schedule

Schedule your work, so you don’t get bogged down the whole day on one aspect. Being prepared with everything before filming saves you lots of time and effort. Set a specific time for filming every day so that you do not find yourself with errands to run

Keep Your Equipment in Tiptop Shape

Faulty equipment can throw all your plans into disarray, causing delays in updating your content. Avoid the frustration of running around looking for alternatives by ensuring your gear is in good functioning order. You may consider getting a spare camera or a good smartphone to act as a backup.

Get a Good Quality Video Editor

Post-production is just as important, if not more important than the actual filming. This last process is what determines what your audience sees, and if done with a shoddy editor, it doesn’t matter in the least if your video filming was of the best quality.

Don’t Film Everything

If you’re filming aspects of your life, as many vloggers do, don’t shoot everything. You may waste precious time filming mundane moments and bore your audience. They will most likely jump to the next video. Take the choicest parts during editing and keep your audience hooked.

Look Out For an Opportunity

Other vloggers are covering the same subject as you are on the same platform that you’re on. Look at what they’re putting out and see what you can value add to your product. There will always be an opportunity you can snap up.

Expect Trolls and Criticism

Don’t be put off by criticism; the usual haters are always there. The best videos on Youtube still have some negative comments. Expect the worst, but don’t let it put you down as there will always be a few encouraging comments too. Instead of taking affront, take this as a motivation to do better.
